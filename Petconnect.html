PetConnect

Project Overview

This case study was created in Winter 2021 Design at UCI’s Project Teams. ​​​​​​New and existing pet owners have a hard time accessing pet related resources and information when obtaining a pet, possibly supporting unethical organizations unknowingly. ​PetConnect is an application that conveniently provides pet resources and connects potential pet owners to pet businesses on a singular source.

Duration

January 2021 - March 2021

10 weeks

Role

UX/UI Designer and Researcher

Tools

Team

Research

Competitive Analysis

We started our research with competitive analysis. For competitive analysis, we found other websites that had similar use as our application. The columns represents the websites we care doing our competitive analysis with and the row represents a feature present in the applications we evaluated. Through these analysis, we analyzed what these websites lacked and what we could do different things to be unique.

Research

Initial Survey​​​​​​​

To begin the preliminary research, my team decided to send out an initial survey through google forms. After 59 responses, we found that most people got their pets through adoption, breeders, or pet stores. ‍ To further understand how the application can maximize a good experience for users, we asked for suggestions on what helpful features should be implemented to the applications that have not been mentioned yet. Unique suggestions that we took into consideration were a quiz that suggests a specific pet that accommodates potential owners’ lifestyles based on their answers. A feature to screen pet businesses to decrease the chances of working with an untrustworthy unethical breeder or adoption center.

Define

Problem Statment

New and existing pet owners can access resources all across the Internet (not from a singular source) and can search for pet businesses, such as breeders and adoption centers, using a regular search engine.

Ideation

User Flow

Before creating our initial wireframes, we brainstormed the user flow of both pet owners and businesses. As potential pet owners, users can access many resources such as finding pet businesses, VetMap, and messages. For business owners, users can promote their profile by adding new pets, editing their profile, and reaching out to potential pet owners.

Research

Sketches

Looking at the user flow for references, we planned out PetConnect’s initial wireframe easier. The initial wireframe helped us plan out the top navigation bar, pages, and login screen. On the top bar, users are able to access settings and notifications. The home, resources, messages, VetMap, and profile page are navigated through the bottom navigation bar. For the login screen, there are two options: new users will sign up as potential pet or business owners and existing owners will log in with their information.

Design

Color and Typography

For our application, we finalized a style guide to use as we move on to high-fidelity prototypes. Our team decided to choose round typography and warm colors to provide a friendly atmosphere for our users when they use PetConnect.

Iterate

Usability Testing

I was in charge of creating the notifications, settings, home, resources, VetMap, messages, user profile pages. After we finished our high-fidelity prototype, we have conducted 7 usability testing to get feedback on our application. Key changes that we concluded from the usability testing are:‍

Error Prevention

When signing up or logging in, text fields and buttons were the same color. To prevent errors made by the user, we utilized color to reflect differences between elements.

Provide better feedback

There was a lot of confusion after the user took the pet quiz. To resolve this confusion, we display and have a popup of the result after the quiz. Another confusion is using checkmarks for the quiz, which makes the user assume that there can be multiple answers. To make sure the user knows it is a one-answer quiz, we turned the checkboxes into a dotted circle.

Reduce confusion

For the post feature, we wanted to make it as simple as possible. However, the simplicity of the post feature made a lot of the users confused and did not know what exactly it did. To fix this conclusion, we added more detailed inputs and redefined the meaning of the post.

Have help and documentation

Since we did have a lot of unique features, first-time users might be overwhelmed and not know where to start or how to use some of the features. To accommodate this, we have added a help popup where our mascot, Zuko, explains step by step what each feature is to the user.

Solution

Final Designs

For our final design we created an onboarding process that allows users to create an account by inputting your personal information. After they are finished creating an account, take a quiz that will determine what pet is best suited for them. On pages, users can gain access to numerous pet resources to help them easily settle in their pet life; search the nearest veterinarian near them with VetMaps in urgent situations; and easily connect with pet businesses to communicate their needs. Finally, users can update their information on the profile page to filter posts to their preferences while letting pet businesses know what their are looking for.

Reflection

Initially, we had many ideas when we were planning and realized that we couldn’t implement everything. With the numerous features, we were able to implement, we realized our flows were very confusing and weren’t working out during usability testing. Since there was a lot of confusion during usability testing, we had to make any changes. Some key takeaways I learned from this case study is to be more organized and consistent because it is important to plan first rather than jumping in and having a lot to fix later. For our next steps, I want to be able to do more usability testing and get more user feedback to improve our designs. Review the flow by adding clearer icons, new overlaps, card sorting, and making sure our logic makes sense to our users. Although this was my first time working on a case study and designing, I felt very enlighten and gained so many ideas on how to improve my designing skills when I tackle my next project!
