Abound Food Care

Project Overview

I had the opportunity to join Commit the Change (CTC), a student-led organization at UCI with a mission of creating high-quality software for non-profit organizations while providing meaningful opportunities for students to develop tech with purpose. During the 2022 school year, I worked with a team of talented CTC designers and Abound Food Care (AFC) admins to create a volunteer management system to better ease AFC's pain points regarding managing volunteers and events.

Research

Competitive Analysis

With our user target consisting of volunteers and admins, we decided to further our research by conducting a competitive analysis to understand what already existed in the market and the elements our competitors offered and lacked. The competitive analysis helped conclude that most of our competitors provided tools to efficiently manage employees and volunteers but lacked a user-friendly interface for non-tech savvy users and increase the learning curve that goes into using their systems.

Define

Problem Statement

Abound Food Care (AFC) is a non-profit organization that helps reduce food waste and insecurity by giving excess edible food to communities in need rather than landfills. To achieve their values and goals over the years, AFC has coordinated multiple events; however, problems have arisen with scheduling and organizing their volunteers with inconsistent availability.

How might we help AFC by designing a volunteer management system to better connect with their volunteers and engage them in corporate events?

Ideation

Main Objectives

As a result of our user interviews with the admin of Abound Food Care, we created a Requirements Document to focus on the following features for our admin and volunteer user base. The primary admin features consist of event and volunteer database management systems while being able to approve volunteer hour logs. The volunteer feature prioritized creating a volunteer profile, signing up for events, and submitting volunteer hours. When designing, we always reiterate to ensure the necessary tools for AFC admins are implemented without overwhelming experience or interface.

Ideation

Information Architecture

To brainstorm and gasp what type of system components the system needs to accommodate users and users' responses to those actions, we began laying out the information architecture for both volunteers and admin users. For both account, users will be able to create an account through an onboarding progress-- this account will allow users to go back and access their information.


Admin

Admin's responsibilities are to manage volunteer events that are happening within Abound Food Care along with keeping track of the availability and information of the participating volunteers through their portals. Theses tasks can be separated to three pages: Volunteer, Waivers/Forms, and Event. Within those pages, multiple tasks can be performed by the admins. On the volunteer page, admins will be available to conveniently view all volunteer's information such as their availability, demographics, and training progress. In the Waiver/Forms section, admins will be able to upload and view waivers through filters. Lastly, the Events page will allow admins to create and view events, along with sending important updates for volunteer in that event group.


Volunteer

To make the information exchange progress easy for both sides, we have created the Profile, Waivers/Forms, Events, and Hours pages. In the Profile page, volunteers will enter and edit their information as well as upload their Waivers/Forms. Volunteers will have access to the Events page where they can sign up to volunteer and view event details. After going to the event, they will be able to input their hours they volunteer for a specific event and view them anytime in the future.

Iterate

Usability Testing

We conducted three rounds of usability testing with 13 participants-- two rounds testing mid-fidelity wireframes and one round for testing the final product. We focused more on the mid-fidelity to be time-efficient while making gradual changes to reduce the modifications we had to make during the given timeframe. After each round, we remember to iterate changes to our wireframes accordingly to improve any confusions and pain points the AFC admins and volunteers had.

Admin Updates

Prototyping an intuitive heat map

Based on admin user feedback, the heat map was very confusing to use, especially for those who have never seen one before. As a result, we prototyped the heat map to display the volunteers available when they hovered over specific times, along with an information icon to provide further guidance.

Add comments when declining a volunteer’s hours

To improve communication between the admin and volunteer, we provided a feature that allows admins to give feedback to volunteers and communicate why their hours are being declined. We decided to make this feature displayed as a modal to minimize admins’ memory load and not creating a complicated step to do this task.

Volunteer Updates

Multiple ways to find uploaded waivers

Some volunteer participants could not find their previously signed waivers under their user profiles. Therefore, we made finding waivers more accessible by allowing users to view their signed waivers on its event page as well.

Visibility of event status

To let users know the event status, we needed to update the event page to indicate whether the event is coming up, ongoing, or has ended along with whether users are signed up for the event. Given the current state of the event, we needed to create a flow that reflects the progress in time and the actions the users need to perform.

Reflection

Abound Food Care (AFC) was the first project I’ve worked on for a specific customer with specific and immediate goals, wants, and needs in the system. This project was insightful because of the communication skills I developed while collaborating with other designers and admins of AFC. Most of my work has been conceptual; working with real-life customers and designing around the given requirement was very fun to problem solve. Some key takeaways are understanding the importance of user testing and being considerate of deployment. Along with focusing on our user base’s pain points, having iterations of usability testing ensure improvements in functionalities and spotting crucial information that was not communicated during the requirements elicitation. Additionally, I realized how important it was to involve developers in the design process since some tools may not be feasible to implement within a limited period.
