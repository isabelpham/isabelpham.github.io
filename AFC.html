Abound Food Care

Project Overview

I had the opportunity to join Commit the Change (CTC), a student-led organization at UCI with a mission of creating high-quality software for non-profit organizations while providing meaningful opportunities for students to develop tech with purpose. During the 2022 school year, I worked with a team of talented CTC designers and Abound Food Care (AFC) admins to create a volunteer management system to better ease AFC's pain points regarding managing volunteers and events.

Research

Competitive Analysis

With our user base consisting of volunteers and admins, we decided to further our research by conducting a competitive analysis to understand what already existed in the market and the elements our competitors offered and lacked. The competitive analysis helped conclude that most of our competitors provided tools to efficiently manage employees and volunteers but lacked a user-friendly interface for non-tech savvy users because of the learning curve that goes into using their systems.

Problem Statment

Abound Food Care (AFC) is a non-profit organization that helps reduce food waste and insecurity by giving excess edible food to communities in need rather than landfills. To achieve their values and goals over the years, AFC has coordinated multiple events; however, problems have arisen with scheduling and organizing their volunteers with inconsistent availability.

How might we help AFC by designing a volunteer management system to better connect with their volunteers and engage them in corporate events?

Ideation

Main Objectives

As a result of our research, we decided to focus on the following features for our admin and volunteer user base. The primary volunteer feature prioritized creating a volunteer profile, signing up for events, and submitting volunteer hours. The primary admin features consist of event and volunteer database management systems while being able to approve volunteer hour logs. When designing, we always reiterate to ensure the necessary tools for AFC admins are implemented without overwhelming experience or interface.

Iterate

Usability Testing

We conducted three rounds of usability testing with 13 participants, two on mid-fidelity wireframes and one for the final product. We focused more on testing mid-fidelity to be time-efficient while making gradual changes to reduce the modifications we had to make during the given timeframe. After each round, we remember to iterate changes to our wireframes to improve any confusions and pain points the AFC admins and volunteers had. After testing, we made changes to both admin and volunteer designs accordingly.

Admin Updates

Prototyping an intuitive heat map

- Based on admin user feedback, the heat map was very confusing to use, especially for those who have never seen one before.

- Prototyped the heat map to display the volunteers available when they hovered over specific times.

- Added an information icon to provide further guidance.

Add comments when declining a volunteer’s hours

- The feature provides the ability for admins to give feedback to volunteers. The volunteer’s information is displayed at the top of the modal to minimize admins’ memory load.

Volunteer Updates

Multiple ways to find uploaded waivers

- Some volunteer participants could not find their previously signed waivers under their user profiles.

- Therefore, we made finding waivers more accessible by allowing users to view their signed waivers on its event page as well.

Visibility of event status

- We needed to update the event page given whether the event is coming up, ongoing, or has ended and whether users are signed up for the event.

- Given the current state of the event, we needed to create a flow that reflects the progress in time and the actions the users need to perform.

Reflection

Abound Food Care (AFC) was the first project I’ve worked on for a specific customer with specific and immediate goals, wants, and needs in the system. This project was insightful because of the communication skills I developed while collaborating with other designers and admins of AFC. Most of my work has been conceptual; working with real-life customers and designing around the given requirement was very fun to problem solve. Some key takeaways are understanding the importance of user testing and being considerate of deployment. Along with focusing on our user base’s pain points, having iterations of usability testing ensure improvements in functionalities and spotting crucial information that was not communicated during the requirements elicitation. Additionally, I realized how important it was to involve developers in the design process since some tools may not be feasible to implement within a limited period.
