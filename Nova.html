Nova

Project Overview

Nova seeks to solve overlooked gift-giving challenges, with the goal to serve as a platform for users to create and share wishlists, increasing everyone’s chances of giving and receiving the perfect gift. It also allows friends and family members of the user to have an easier time picking a gift without clashing with someone else's choice by notifying gift-givers if someone already bought the item — without telling the wishlist owner.

Research

User Interviews

We interviewed 7 participants in total. We asked the following questions to understand the experience participants have during their current process of gift-giving.

1. What are your frustrations when it comes to gift-giving a friend?

2. What would you like your gift-giver to know when purchasing your gift?

3. What are some resources you use now to help you with organizing gift-giving?

4. Recall a time when you had a difficult time choosing a gift for a friend. What was that experience like?

Research

Affinity Map

After collecting the responses of each participant, we organized them into categories to help analyze the groups for patterns.

Research

Insights

Research

Empathy Map

To further understand our target audiences’ frustration, analyze participants’ answers in our user interviews into an empathy map. After analyzing the data we got back from the surveys, we are able to understand our users more and analyze our empathy map, allowing us to focus on what the user needs.

Research

Personas

Using the affinity map, we created a persona to represent our main target demographics. Our persona celebrates various of gift-giving holidays annually. Struggling to pick the perfect gift, Shannon’s goal is to find a trouble-free alternative to google sheets to keep her friend’s traditions alive. Having personas allows us to have a reference of users throughout the designing process to remind us what our user’s needs and goals are every step of the way of the designing process.

Research

Scenarios

When designing, I wanted to have my team pay attention to a more crucial pain point we have missed that the user will have during the gift-giving process. I want to emphasize that allowing users to see the availability status of items was a crucial feature we needed to implement to reduce the chances of gift clashes. When demonstrating it to my team, I decided to create a scenario to walk them through it visually.

Define

Problem Statement

Gift-giving can transform fun gift-giving holidays into stressful situations. It can be difficult to ask others what they might want without spoiling the surprise or avoiding a clash with someone else’s gift choice.

How might we reduce stress on users during the gift-giving progress, while being able to maintain the surprise to the recipient?

Ideation

User Journey

After researching our user’s pain points through our empathy map and personas. We brainstormed solutions to reduce our user’s stress. After selecting those we created user flows to see what steps the user would take when using these features, allowing us to design the flow in a way that is most intuitive to users.

Research

Sketches

Using our user flows, we created sketches to visualize our wireframes and have a basic outline of what features and functionalities our application would have.

Research

Mid-Fidelity

After creating sketches, we translated our low-fidelity sketches into mid-fidelity wireframes where are able to visualize our functionalities in more detail along with prototyping them to interact and follow along with how the web flow will work together when the user uses our Nova. We decided to test our mid-fidelity wireframes through usability testing to get feedback from testers that have the same character traits as our target audience to check if our flows and features make sense to them.

Design

Color and Typography

When creating Nova’s branding, I wanted to incorporate an intuitive design that allows users to quickly speculate on the purpose of our application. Since Nova is a wishlist application, I wanted to incorporate stars to associate it with wishing stars. Use purple as our primary color because it doesn’t clash with a specific holiday– for example, pink for Valentine’s or red for Christmas.

Iterate

Usability Testing

Overall, users generally felt that Nova was a usable and intuitive application. All users reacted positively to the overall look and feel of the application and expressed interest in the product goal in general. However, despite most users having a high completion and low error rate, all users struggled with poor usability at some point in their evaluation. Some common pain points amongst users of all experience levels were:

Confusion within system status when adding an item

When adding an item, we replaced the search bar with a modal that accepts input for various fields, such as the item name, price, link, etc. Now, the system only accepts adding one item at a time, a much more straightforward flow that removes previously ambiguous states.

Low affordability in footers and card-like widgets

We made some elements look more interactive by changing the “Ask a question” footer to a more affordable button because it was not apparent that the footer is interactive to the majority of the testers. Additionally, the process of having the “Ask a question” on the general wishlist would have more unnecessary steps, resulting in us moving the button to the page with the specific item the user would like to question.

Ambiguity in text and labels

We made labels more descriptive across the interface, such as changing “date” to “event date” to communicate that the event date consists of a single day rather than a range of dates.

Solution

Final Designs

The main function of Nova allows users to create multiple wishlists and add items to them. As a user with a wishlist creator account, we have created pages where users can view and create their wishlist, along with viewing and adding an item. Nova will allow users to access wishlists anonymously. As a guest, users do not have to create an account to view wishlists. Guest accounts are allowed to see if an item is taken or available and navigate to the item’s external link to buy it. To further assist the user in gifting the perfect gift, anonymous chats are implemented so the user can ask users anonymously about the item.

WishList Creator

Wishlist page

For the wishlist page, users can create wishlists. When creating a wishlist, inputs will be asked to give buyers a summary of the wishlist content. Users will be prompted to give the wishlist a name, the date of the gift-giving event, the price range of the items, and a description of the wishlist.

WishList Creator

Wishlist’s item page

When the user clicks on a wishlist, a variety of items that the wishlist owner has added will appear, along with descriptions the user inputted when they created the wishlist. On this page, the user is allowed to edit descriptions, share, or add an item to the wishlist.

WishList Creator

Item detail page

Users will be able to view item details and check if the information is correct. Users will be able to edit, delete, and share the item detail page with others.

Guest

Wishlist page

For the wishlist page, guests can view existing wishlists and the summary of the wishlist content that the creator provided. On this page, guests can view the name, date of the gift-giving event, price range of the items, and a description of the wishlist.

Guest

Item detail and anonymous chat page

Users will be able to view item details that the wishlist creator has provided when they click on a specific item. Here, guest accounts can verify the size, color, style, and additional information on the item the wishlist creator wants. Once the guest decides on buying the item, they will press the “view item” button to be navigated to the item’s external site. Additionally, users can ask the wishlist creator an anonymous question about the item and the response will be sent to their email.

Reflection

This project was unique for me since it is a system I would utilize. The biggest struggle during the gift-giving holidays is finding a gift that my friends would like but It’s hard to ask them for specifics without ruining the surprise. It is interesting to find myself more passionate about the subject since I can relate to it more. However, it was challenging because of this; I could be easy to make assumptions since I think I was part of the target demographic. From this experience, I understand that making assumptions can hurt potential users since I only represent one type of user in the vast target group– I would have to keep into consideration of users who have a different level of experience with technology. One takeaway I got from this was the importance of UX writing. In many projects, I was more focused on if the flow and design made sense to users, but in this project a lot of usability participants have pointed out that the usage of texts was ambiguous, affecting their experience when using Nova. Therefore, I found that usability testing was important and I found common struggles users had during the usability testing process, because of this I was able to resolve them and make the application easier for a diverse audience to use.
