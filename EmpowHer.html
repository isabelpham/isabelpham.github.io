EmpowHer

Project Overview

This case study was created in Spring 2021 Design at UCI’s Project Teams. ​​​​​​​Finding a safe space for women that includes advice and information varies across different platforms, resulting in difficulties when finding specific information. Even if that platform does provide these resources, users often have to pay a subscription to join. EmpowHer is an application that provides a platform for women to educate or empower other women through event webinars and forums.

Duration

Research

Competitive Analysis

We researched many different products that had similar purposes or features to EmpowHer and compare them to what we have to offer. The websites we mainly looked towards were: Reddit, a website with thousands of user-run forums; Peanut, a network for mothers to connect to each other and discuss motherhood; Girlboss, a website that provides resources and community to help women in their personal and professional development. Two of the main features we were looking to include were creating a community forum for users and providing useful resources. We checked these applications to see their functions, see what they lacked, and improve it in our application.

Research

User Survey

To begin the preliminary research, my team decided to send out an initial survey through google forms. After 59 responses, we found that most people got their pets through adoption, breeders, or pet stores. ‍ To further understand how the application can maximize a good experience for users, we asked for suggestions on what helpful features should be implemented to the applications that have not been mentioned yet. Unique suggestions that we took into consideration were a quiz that suggests a specific pet that accommodates potential owners’ lifestyles based on their answers. A feature to screen pet businesses to decrease the chances of working with an untrustworthy unethical breeder or adoption center.

Research

Empathy Map

We collectively built an empathy map by brainstorming some of the feelings, goals, tasks, influences, and pain points of users. This helped influence our creation of user personas by highlighting the user’s potential needs. For example, when building the empathy map, we realized one of the most major goals of EmpowHer is for users to meet and talk to people with similar experiences as them which was then emphasized greatly as a need and goal in both of the personas. One pain point we ran into was the possibility of people getting offended or triggered due to the discussion of a sensitive topic, which prompted us to include the tagging feature within forum posts so that users can filter out topics that may trigger or upset them.

Research

Empathy Map

Using the information collected from our surveys and empathy maps, we were able to create 2 distinct types of users. With both of these personas, we wanted to provide a community and highlight a variety of different topics for women of different ages and different stages in life.

Define

Problem Statment

With Covid restrictions being mandated, it is difficult to find a safe space to learn about rights or other information that is not through in school or parents. Also, it limits the resources women get access to find their community and support one another. With all of these conflicting elements during the hard times of the pandemic, it can decrease women’s mental health or put them at risk because of the lack of knowledge.

How do we design a platform with the resources women need to increase their safety and mental health.

Ideation

Information Architecture

Before creating our initial wireframes, we brainstormed the information architecture of the average user of EmpowHer. By focusing on organizing, structuring, and labeling content in an effective and sustainable way helps us to help users find information and complete tasks, which would help us plan the initial wireframes easily.

Design

Lo-fi wireframes

After brainstorming the information architecture, we created low-fidelity wireframes to plan out our application. The initial wireframe helped us plan out the sign up, archive, join the talk, home, and event pages. To allow users to navigate to past events, we created a page that contained all of the resources for that page so the user can easily access it through the archive page. We designed a home page that allows users to access information to current and upcoming events. If a user is interested in one of the events, they can signup through our event page. To keep them engage in the community, we created a forums page called "Join the Talk" that allows users to post questions or concerns they might have and give other users who have the information for the topic a chance to answer them.

Design

Mid-fi wireframes

Visually planning the layout, we created mid-fidelity wireframes to go into detail on where the features should be. In this project, I was in charge of webinar, profile, forum page. On the webinar page, users can find more information on the events that they are interested in. Users can access activity history and interesting events on their profiles. Forums allow users to interact with other users and spread their knowledge with one another.

Design

Color and Typography

Our team decided to choose round typography and cute colors to provide a safe atmosphere for our users when they are using EmpowHer.

Iterate

Usability Testing

We have decided to do usability testing on our mid-fidelity wireframe so we have a better idea of what works and doesn’t before finalizing our application. We conducted 8 usability testing to get feedback on our application. Key changes that we concluded from the usability testing are:‍

A/B Testing

When we were making our wireframe, we were stuck on which organization of information we liked the most. The two options we were stuck on were list or card view. With 75% of our users preferring a card view over a list.

Resolving Confusion

There was a lot of confusion on our ad post due to the lack of headers. On top of added headers, we also changed it so the post was on the left because people usually read from left to right.‍

Final Designs

Reflection

Due to a time limit resulting in us having to pick a topic we were not familiar with, we felt like more time should’ve been spent on solidifying our idea and settling on the right idea before we created the survey. On topics that we are not familiar with, it is important to do a lot of research before designing the application; research helped us understand why we were doing this and how to make our design more effective. Another thing I learned was that brainstorming together on the user flow helped us be on the same page. The next steps for EmpowHer are to conduct more usability testing because we only tested our mid-fidelity; we hope that more testing will resolve most of our potential issues. During the time of creating this case study, we were planning to create a static website, due to time constraints, we were not able to do so-- we plan on creating that in our next steps as well. Lastly, we hope to publish the website and market it to relevant communities, hoping to help them. ​​​​​​​
