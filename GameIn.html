GameIn Internship

Project Overview

GameIn is a customer-centric gaming platform that supports matchmaking between eSports teams and organizations (or other companies) to connect. GameIn is a web application that provides services. First, GameIn provides team finding which allows the organization to select and filter to find the best matching team. Secondly, GameIn gives teams and organization certification that verifies them to ensure a more secure process of matchmaking. Additionally, GameIn aims to create a standard for the matchmaking process throughout the eSports ecosystem, targeting amateur to professional athletes and organizations. Within the website, GameIn ensures that each eSports team and organization on the platform follows the standards that are aligned with GameIn’s values. The history of achievement would be transparently presented to the organization. This decreases the organization’s process of research and increases matchmaking. It would maximize the financial support of the eSports team with a shorter time period for the team research.

Reflection

During my internship at GameIn, I had a great time designing and aiding with the front-end development of their product. I was very excited to be on the team since I was interested in the gaming industry, but to my surprise, there were different parts of the industry I did not know. Coming in with little knowledge of the problems in obtaining sponsorships within the Esports community led me to begin researching to familiarize myself with it. In the middle of the internship, I got the opportunity to collaborate with the software engineers on the team to help with the front end of the product. Two takeaways I have upon completing the internship are:


Don't be afraid to state your opinion.

Due to the strict deadline GameIn had for us, my coworkers have suggested we skip the wireframing portion of the project. At first, everyone was leaning toward that idea, which made me scared to speak up. After a few moments of recollecting my confidence, I expressed my worries that coding up the prototype would take more time to create and fix compared to wireframes on Figma. After communicating my concerns, the team agreed to make the Figma wireframes first. Throughout our project, we made many iterations of changes to our user interface. If I had never spoken about my worries, we would have had to jeopardize our deadline with the extensive changes to the code.


Get clarification.

Although I had some programming experience, our team used a framework and preprocessor language (React and Sass) I had no experience with, making the transition to the front-end team challenging. Not only did I have to familiarize myself with the syntax, but comprehended what the software developers' team developed so far. After reading the documentation and requesting a code walkthrough with one of the developers, I was confident in beginning work for the pages I was responsible for. As a result of asking questions, I was able to create components for the Create Teams, Dashboard, and Message Center pages at the end of the deadline.
